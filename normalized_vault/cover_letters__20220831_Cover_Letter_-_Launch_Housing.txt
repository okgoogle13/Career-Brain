Dear Erin, 
My friend Antonie loves working for Launch and brought this opportunity to my attention. It’s 
been a long time since I’ve felt positive emotions after reading a position description so I’m 
grateful for being reminded that its possible! 
My name is Nishant. I am a Diploma of Community Services graduate with a background in 
project management and significant lived experience as a queer, neuro-diverse, gender-diverse, 
person of colour. 
I had to move away from the corporate sector. My last role on an ethics project for bank for 
society’s richest broke my heart. It opened my eyes to the vastness of the structural inequalities 
and inequities in society and it culminated in an existential crisis, which included being violently 
assaulted, addiction, and 4 longggg months of homelessness.  
Thankfully, I survived, and I now know how strongly I feel about social injustices. Working for an 
organization that is affecting meaningful change is critical for me as I start this new chapter. 
I’ve always been empathic and an excellent listener. I’m a quick learner, and the problem 
solving and strongly believe the time/task management skills I developed via 7 years in project 
delivery are transferable.  Although I’m a relative newbie, my studies have prepared me for 
working in the social welfare sector, and my lived experience of discrimination and 
intersectionality has given me a profound appreciation of the layers and complexity in assessing 
clients’ needs. 
I would love to be one of those people that looks forward to coming to work! I believe I could 
be a really fit for this role.  
I hope to hear from you soon. 
 
Kind Regards
