# Resume Best Practices & Style Guide (Australian Context)

This guide outlines the principles for crafting an effective, ATS-friendly, and impactful master resume specifically for the Australian job market, with a focus on Community Services and Social Work.

---

## 1. Overall Philosophy
* **Targeted & Relevant:** Customise for each role. Every point should demonstrate your fit.
* **Achievement-Oriented:** Focus on quantifiable results and impact, not just responsibilities.
* **Clear & Concise:** Easy to read and digest. Avoid jargon where plain language suffices.
* **Professional & Ethical:** Reflects the high ethical standards of the community services sector.
* **ATS Friendly:** Optimise for Applicant Tracking Systems.

---

## 2. Australian Context Specifics

* **Personal Information:**
    * **DO NOT INCLUDE:** Photos, date of birth, age, marital status, religion, nationality, or detailed home address (city/state/postcode or city/country is sufficient). These are generally discouraged to prevent unconscious bias.
    * **INCLUDE:** Full Name, Phone Number, Professional Email Address, LinkedIn Profile URL.
* **Resume Length:**
    * **Graduates/Entry-Level (0-2 years):** Max 1 page.
    * **Mid-Career (3-10 years):** 1-2 pages.
    * **Senior/Executive (10+ years):** 2-3 pages (maximum of 4-5 pages for highly complex, extensive executive careers with significant publications/boards).
* **References:** Always state "References available upon request" at the end. Do not include referee details on the resume itself.
* **Pronouns:** While summaries can be implicit third-person, in bullet points, it's acceptable and often clearer to use "I" to describe your specific achievements (e.g., "I managed...", "I collaborated...").

---

## 3. Key Sections & Content Guidelines

### A. Contact Information (Top of Resume)
* **Must Include:** Full Name, Phone Number (Australian format), Professional Email Address, LinkedIn Profile URL.
* **Optional:** Portfolio/Personal Website URL (if relevant, e.g., for program design, research).

### B. Professional Summary (2-4 sentences)
* **Purpose:** A compelling introduction to your professional self, highlighting your unique value and career objectives within the Australian Community Services/Social Work context.
* **Content:**
    * Years of experience in the sector.
    * 2-3 core competencies/areas of expertise (e.g., "Client-centred case management," "Trauma-informed practice").
    * Highlight 1-2 significant achievements or unique value propositions.
    * Use keywords relevant to the target role and sector (e.g., NDIS, Child Protection).
* **Style:** Write in a concise, impactful manner.

### C. Core Competencies / Skills
* **Categorization:** Group skills into logical categories (e.g., Clinical Skills, Casework & Support, Community Engagement, Administrative & Compliance, Software & Tools).
* **Content:** List specific skills, relevant legislation (e.g., NDIS), methodologies (e.g., Trauma-Informed Care), software, and professional registrations (e.g., AASW Membership, WWCC).
* **Keywords:** Crucial for ATS matching. Incorporate keywords from job descriptions and the `_Keywords_AU_CommunityServices.md` file.
* **Order:** List most relevant/proficient skills first within categories.

### D. Professional Experience (Reverse Chronological Order)
* **Format for Each Role:**
    * **Job Title:** Bold and prominent.
    * **Company/Organisation Name, City, State/Territory:** (e.g., "Melbourne, VIC" or "Sydney, NSW").
    * **Employment Dates:** (Month Year – Month Year, or Present).
* **Bullet Points (3-5 per role):**
    * **Start with an Action Verb:** (Refer to `_ActionVerbs_AU_SocialWork.md`).
    * **Focus on Achievements & Impact:** How did your work benefit clients, programs, or the organisation?
    * **Quantify:** Use numbers, percentages, caseload sizes, number of programs delivered, funding secured, client outcomes achieved. (Refer to `_QuantifiableAchievementExamples_AU.md`).
    * **Tailor:** Adjust emphasis based on the relevance of the achievement to the specific job.
    * **Keywords:** Naturally weave in sector-specific terminology and relevant legislation/frameworks.

### E. Education
* **Format:** Degree, Major, Institution Name, City, State/Territory, Graduation Date (or Expected Graduation Date).
* **Include:** Professional development, short courses, mandatory training relevant to the sector (e.g., First Aid, MHFA, NDIS Worker Training).
* **Optional:** GPA (if 3.5+ and recent graduate), relevant coursework.

### F. Additional Sections (If Applicable & Relevant)
* **Certifications & Licenses:** Specific industry certifications (e.g., Accredited Mental Health Social Worker, Cert IV Disability).
* **Professional Memberships/Registrations:** AASW, ACWA, WWCC (state-specific name).
* **Awards & Recognition:** Professional awards, internal recognition, scholarships.
* **Publications & Presentations:** Research, conference presentations, workshops delivered.
* **Volunteer Experience:** Highly valued in this sector, especially if demonstrating relevant skills.
* **Key Projects:** Significant projects or initiatives led or contributed to.
* **Language Skills:** If relevant to supporting diverse client groups.

---

## 4. Formatting & Readability
* **Clean Layout:** Use a clean, professional, and easy-to-read layout.
* **Font:** Use standard, professional fonts (e.g., Calibri, Arial, Verdana, Georgia) between 10-12pt for body text, 14-18pt for headings.
* **Margins:** Standard 0.5 to 1 inch margins.
* **White Space:** Crucial for readability; avoid dense text blocks.

---

## 5. ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Optimization
* **Keywords:** Incorporate keywords from job descriptions and this guide naturally throughout the resume.
* **Standard Headings:** Use conventional section headings (e.g., "Professional Experience," "Skills," "Education").
* **Avoid Graphics/Charts for Key Info:** ATS may not read text embedded in images.
* **PDF Format:** Always submit resumes as PDFs unless explicitly asked otherwise, to preserve formatting.

---

## 6. Review & Proofread
* **Accuracy:** Double-check all details.
* **Consistency:** Ensure consistent tense, punctuation, and formatting.
* **Grammar & Spelling:** Proofread meticulously for any errors. Australian English spelling should be used (e.g., "analyse" not "analyze").
