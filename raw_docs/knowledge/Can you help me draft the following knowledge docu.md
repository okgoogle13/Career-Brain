## 1. ATS and AI Resume Best Practices Guide

**Purpose:**
Summarizes how ATS systems parse resumes, key formatting rules, pitfalls to avoid, and Australian/NDIS-specific considerations.

**Contents:**

- **How ATS Systems Work**
    - ATS software scans resumes for keywords, experience, and qualifications.
    - Resumes are parsed into structured data for recruiter review.
    - Complex formatting (tables, columns, graphics) can cause parsing errors.
- **Key Formatting Rules**
    - Use single-column, standard layouts.
    - Avoid tables, images, and fancy fonts.
    - Use standard section headers (e.g., Summary, Skills, Experience, Education, Certifications).
    - Save as .docx or .pdf for best compatibility.
- **Common Pitfalls**
    - Overuse of acronyms without explanation.
    - Missing or inconsistent dates.
    - Lack of relevant keywords from job descriptions.
- **Australian-Specific ATS Advice**
    - Follow SEEK and LinkedIn best practices for Australian resumes.
    - Emphasize local certifications and compliance (e.g., NDIS Worker Screening, First Aid).
- **NDIS/Community Services Compliance**
    - Clearly state NDIS Worker Screening status.
    - Highlight relevant certifications (e.g., Certificate III/IV in Community Services).
    - Use sector-standard terminology (e.g., person-centered care, case management, advocacy)[^1][^2].
- **Best Practices Checklist**
    - Use clear, concise language.
    - Tailor each resume to the job description.
    - Proofread for spelling and grammar errors.
    - Keep length to 2–3 pages.

## 2. Prompt Engineering Handbook

**Purpose:**
Documents advanced prompt engineering techniques and provides sample prompts for resume analysis, keyword extraction, and section generation.

**Contents:**

- **Advanced Prompt Engineering Techniques**
    - **Chain-of-Thought Prompting:** Break down analysis into logical steps (e.g., identify skills, extract achievements, match to job requirements).
    - **Few-Shot Learning:** Use examples to guide the AI in generating desired outputs.
    - **Multi-Criteria Evaluation:** Prompt the AI to evaluate multiple aspects (formatting, keyword density, clarity).
- **Sample Prompts**
    - **Keyword Extraction:**
> "Extract essential skills and qualifications from this community services job description and list them in order of importance."
    - **Summary Crafting:**
> "Write a two-sentence professional summary for a support worker with 5 years of experience in disability support and NDIS coordination."
    - **STAR Method Bullet Points:**
> "Convert the following work experience into three bullet points using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result), focusing on measurable outcomes."
- **Prompt Optimization Tips**
    - Be specific about the desired output format.
    - Use clear, concise language.
    - Iterate and refine prompts based on output quality[^3][^1][^2].

## 3. Australian Community Services Sector Reference

**Purpose:**
Lists common job titles, required certifications, NDIS worker screening, and sector-specific terminology; outlines typical duties and skills.

**Contents:**

- **Common Job Titles**
    - Support Worker
    - Case Manager
    - NDIS Coordinator
    - Disability Support Worker
    - Community Services Worker
- **Required Certifications**
    - Certificate III or IV in Community Services
    - NDIS Worker Screening Check
    - First Aid Certificate
    - Working with Children Check (where applicable)
- **NDIS Worker Screening**
    - Required for all roles involving NDIS participants.
    - Must be current and valid.
- **Sector-Specific Terminology**
    - Person-centered care
    - Case management
    - Advocacy
    - Individualized support plans
    - Cultural competency
- **Typical Duties and Skills**
    - Providing direct care and support to clients.
    - Developing and implementing support plans.
    - Liaising with families, health professionals, and service providers.
    - Maintaining accurate records and documentation.
    - Demonstrating empathy, communication, and problem-solving skills[^1][^2].

## 4. Keyword Repository

**Purpose:**
Maintains a dynamic list of relevant keywords and phrases from live job postings, with synonyms and usage context.

**Contents:**

- **Dynamic Keyword List**
    - Regularly updated from live Australian community services job postings.
    - Includes both hard and soft skills (e.g., "case management," "NDIS compliance," "communication," "teamwork").
- **Synonyms and Context**
    - "Case management" = "client coordination," "service planning"
    - "NDIS compliance" = "NDIS standards," "NDIS regulations"
    - "Person-centered care" = "individualized support," "client-focused care"
- **Usage Guidelines**
    - Embed keywords naturally in bullet points and summaries.
    - Avoid keyword stuffing; focus on relevance and context.
    - Use job description-specific keywords for each application[^1][^2].
- **Example Table**

| Keyword | Synonyms/Alternatives | Example Usage in Resume |

| Case management | Client coordination | Managed caseloads for 30+ clients |
| NDIS compliance | NDIS standards | Ensured all documentation met NDIS standards |
| Person-centered care | Individualized support | Delivered person-centered care plans |

## 5. Legal and Compliance Guidelines

**Purpose:**
Documents Australian anti-discrimination laws, privacy requirements, and data handling best practices.

**Contents:**

- **Anti-Discrimination Laws**
    - **Federal Legislation:**
        - Age Discrimination Act 2004
        - Disability Discrimination Act 1992
        - Racial Discrimination Act 1975
        - Sex Discrimination Act 1984
        - Australian Human Rights Commission Act 1986
    - **State/Territory Laws:**
        - Each state and territory has additional anti-discrimination laws.
    - **Key Protections:**
        - Unlawful to discriminate based on age, disability, race, sex, gender identity, or sexual orientation in employment.
        - Ensure resumes and AI outputs do not contain discriminatory language or requests[^4][^5][^6].
- **Privacy Requirements**
    - **Privacy Act 1988:**
        - Governs collection, use, and disclosure of personal information.
        - Requires consent for data collection and sharing.
        - Must protect sensitive information (e.g., health records, NDIS status).
    - **Best Practices:**
        - Use secure storage and transmission for resume data.
        - Implement role-based access controls.
        - Regularly audit data handling practices[^2][^7][^8].
- **Data Handling Best Practices**
    - **Data Classification:**
        - Label data by sensitivity (e.g., confidential, public).
        - Use automated classification where possible.
    - **Access Control:**
        - Restrict access to sensitive data.
        - Implement logging and monitoring.
    - **Disposal:**
        - Securely delete data when no longer needed.
    - **User Consent:**
        - Obtain explicit consent for data collection and processing.
        - Allow users to opt out or request data deletion[^7][^8][^9].
- **Ethical Considerations**
    - Ensure AI-generated resumes are accurate and not misleading.
    - Allow users to review and edit AI outputs.
    - Be transparent about AI involvement if requested by employers[^9].
