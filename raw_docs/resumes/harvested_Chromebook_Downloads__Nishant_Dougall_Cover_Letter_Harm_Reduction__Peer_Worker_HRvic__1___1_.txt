Subject: Application for General Drug Checking Harm Reduction Peer Worker (Events)
To the Hiring Manager,
I am writing to apply for the General Drug Checking Harm Reduction Peer Worker position. My commitment to 
this work is born from a profound values-driven journey, which saw me transition from a nine-year career in 
finance to pursue systemic, meaningful change in community services. For the past four years, I have 
actively immersed myself in the harm reduction sector, and I hold a deep appreciation for Harm Reduction 
Victoria's pioneering leadership in peer-led advocacy and community building.
My approach is grounded in my own comprehensive lived experience navigating the complexities of 
substance use and accessing services. This provides me with a unique dual perspective: I understand the 
barriers and stigma our community faces because I have faced them, and I know the power of authentic 
peer connection because I have both sought and provided it. This insight was central to my work as a Harm 
Reduction Peer Worker at Thorne Harbour Health, where I facilitated bi-weekly support groups, fostering a 
safe space for meaningful engagement.
My harm reduction experience extends to event-based environments through my work with Thorne Harbour 
Health, where I provided sexual health and substance use harm reduction support at sex-on-premises 
venues. These dynamic, peer-focused settings required me to engage with diverse community members in 
real-time, responsive environments—delivering practical harm reduction information while maintaining the 
non-judgmental, person-centered approach that is essential to effective peer work.
Through this frontline peer work and ongoing community engagement, I have gained extensive, real-world 
knowledge of drug markets, emerging trends, and the particular risks associated with novel psychoactive 
substances. I understand that responding to these challenges requires not only formal knowledge, like that 
from my Alcohol and Other Drug Skill Set certificate, but also the ability to remain calm and effective in 
dynamic environments. My experience supporting over 400 vulnerable clients in high-pressure situations at 
Diamond Valley Community Support has honed my ability to apply de-escalation techniques and person-
led support when it is needed most.
It is an incredibly exciting time for harm reduction in Victoria, and I was thrilled to see the announcement of 
the drug checking implementation trial—a testament to HRVic's integral advocacy. My resume provides 
further detail on my qualifications, and I am very eager to discuss how my unique blend of lived and 
professional experience can contribute to this vital new service.
Thank you for your consideration.
Sincerely,
Nishant Dougall
