## **Artifact 2: STAR-Methodology KSC Responses**

### **KSC 1: "Experience in case management and providing direct support to vulnerable individuals experiencing homelessness or housing instability." (Persona A: Housing Support Worker)**

#### **KSC 1: Situation**

In my role as a Housing Support Worker at City Outreach Mission, I was responsible for a caseload of approximately 15-20 clients at any given time. These individuals were experiencing complex challenges, including primary homelessness, chronic health issues, and histories of trauma, making consistent engagement difficult.

#### **KSC 1: Task**

My core task was to provide holistic, end-to-end case management. This involved building trust, assessing immediate and long-term needs, developing client-led case plans, and connecting clients to the appropriate services to help them secure and maintain stable housing.

#### **KSC 1: Action**

I actioned this by first using an assertive outreach model to engage clients in their own environment, building rapport through consistent and non-judgmental support. I conducted comprehensive biopsychosocial assessments using the organisation's approved framework and collaborated with each client to create an Individual Support Plan with clear, achievable goals. I then actively advocated for my clients, making referrals to and liaising with a network of services including GPs, mental health support, Centrelink, and various Specialist Homelessness Services (SHS). I meticulously documented all interactions, assessments, and progress in the Client Management System (CMS) to ensure compliance and continuity of care.

#### **KSC 1: Result**

Through this structured and empathetic approach, I successfully supported 8 clients in moving from primary homelessness into long-term, stable accommodation over the past year. Furthermore, by maintaining detailed and up-to-date case files, I ensured that when I was on leave, my colleagues could seamlessly continue support, leading to a 95% client engagement rate and positive feedback from both clients and the management team on the quality of my case management.

### **KSC 2: "An ability to appropriately use lived experience of recovery to build trust and support the engagement of clients in an Alcohol and Other Drugs (AOD) setting." (Persona B: AOD Peer Support Worker)**

#### **KSC 2: Situation**

While working as an AOD Peer Support Worker at the Community Recovery Centre, I frequently encountered clients who were new to the service and deeply ambivalent about recovery. They often expressed feelings of hopelessness and a strong distrust of "the system" and professional workers, making initial engagement a significant challenge.

#### **KSC 2: Task**

My specific task was to leverage my own lived experience of recovery to bridge this gap, build rapport, and inspire hope. The goal was to make clients feel understood and safe, thereby increasing their willingness to engage with the centre's clinical programs and other supports.

#### **KSC 2: Action**

I achieved this by employing intentional, purposeful self-disclosure. During one-on-one support sessions, if a client shared a specific struggle, such as managing cravings or dealing with stigma, I would (with my supervisor's guidance) share a brief, relevant, and resolved aspect of my own journey. For example, I explained the practical strategies that I found helpful in early recovery, framing it not as advice, but as a shared experience. I always maintained strict professional boundaries, ensuring my disclosures were client-focused, brief, and aimed at normalising their experience and demonstrating that recovery is possible. I also co-facilitated a "Recovery Stories" group where participants could share their journeys in a structured, safe environment.

#### **KSC 2: Result**

This peer-based approach was highly effective. We saw a 40% higher retention rate for new clients who engaged with a Peer Support Worker in their first month compared to those who did not. Clients frequently reported in feedback surveys that connecting with someone with lived experience was the "turning point" that made them feel less alone and more optimistic about their own recovery prospects, leading to greater uptake of our clinical services.

### **KSC 3: "Demonstrated organisational skills, including the ability to contribute to project planning, stakeholder communication, and report drafting." (Persona C: Community Project Officer)**

#### **KSC 3: Situation**

In my position as a Community Services Project Officer at the Better Futures Foundation, I was part of a small team responsible for delivering the "Youth Connect" program, a government-funded initiative aimed at re-engaging at-risk youth with education and employment pathways. The program was approaching its end-of-financial-year reporting deadline, which was critical for securing ongoing funding.

#### **KSC 3: Task**

My task was to support the Project Manager in all aspects of the project's administration and reporting. This involved coordinating stakeholder meetings, drafting communications, collating program data, and contributing to the final progress report for our government funding body.

#### **KSC 3: Action**

To ensure we met our deadlines, I first developed a detailed timeline with key milestones for the reporting period. I used Microsoft Planner to track tasks and assign responsibilities. I was responsible for scheduling and minuting all bi-weekly meetings with our key stakeholders, including local schools and youth employment agencies, and ensuring action items were distributed within 48 hours. I collated all program data from our CRM, including attendance records, participant demographics, and pre- and post-program survey results, and presented this data using clear charts and tables. I then drafted the initial sections of the final report, focusing on program activities and outputs, for the Project Manager to review.

#### **KSC 3: Result**

Due to my systematic organisation and proactive support, the team successfully submitted a high-quality, data-rich report two days ahead of the deadline. The clarity of the data and the well-documented stakeholder engagement were specifically commended by the funding body, and the program's funding was successfully renewed for the following two years, ensuring its vital services could continue.
