# STAR Method Examples - Community Services Work Experience

## Example 1: Crisis Intervention
**Situation:** Responded to after-hours crisis call involving client experiencing acute mental health episode and threatening self-harm
**Task:** Provide immediate safety assessment and de-escalation support while coordinating appropriate emergency response
**Action:** Utilised active listening and trauma-informed communication techniques to build rapport, conducted comprehensive risk assessment, and liaised with police and ambulance services to ensure safe transport to hospital
**Result:** Client safely transported to emergency department without incident; developed safety plan for future crisis situations, resulting in 40% reduction in crisis presentations over following 6 months

## Example 2: Program Development
**Situation:** Identified gap in employment support services for young people exiting out-of-home care in local area
**Task:** Design and implement targeted employment readiness program to address skills gaps and improve job placement outcomes
**Action:** Conducted needs assessment with 25 young people, partnered with local TAFE and employers to develop 8-week program covering resume writing, interview skills, and workplace expectations
**Result:** Delivered program to 40 participants with 75% completion rate and 60% securing employment or further training within 3 months, leading to program expansion across 3 additional sites

## Example 3: Case Management
**Situation:** Assigned complex case involving single mother with 3 children facing homelessness due to domestic violence and rental arrears
**Task:** Develop comprehensive support plan addressing immediate housing needs and longer-term stability goals
**Action:** Coordinated with housing services to secure temporary accommodation, linked client to financial counselling and legal aid, facilitated access to childcare and Centrelink payments, and provided ongoing emotional support
**Result:** Client successfully transitioned to stable long-term housing within 6 weeks, children maintained school attendance, and family remained engaged with support services 12 months later

## Example 4: Community Engagement
**Situation:** Low participation rates in community mental health programs within culturally diverse suburb
**Task:** Increase program engagement and cultural responsiveness to better serve local community needs
**Action:** Conducted community consultation sessions in 4 languages, collaborated with cultural leaders to adapt program content, recruited bilingual peer support workers, and established partnerships with ethnic community organisations
**Result:** Program participation increased by 85% over 12 months, with 90% of participants from diverse cultural backgrounds reporting improved mental health outcomes and community connection

## Example 5: Behaviour Support
**Situation:** Client with intellectual disability exhibiting challenging behaviours in group accommodation setting, affecting housemates and staff safety
**Task:** Implement positive behaviour support plan to reduce incidents while maintaining client's dignity and community participation
**Action:** Collaborated with behaviour specialist to complete functional assessment, identified environmental triggers, developed alternative communication strategies, and provided intensive staff training on de-escalation techniques
**Result:** Reduced behavioural incidents by 70% within 3 months, improved client's social relationships, and prevented potential accommodation breakdown

## Example 6: Advocacy and Rights
**Situation:** Elderly client with dementia being financially exploited by family member, with $15,000 withdrawn from accounts without consent
**Task:** Protect client's rights and financial security while maintaining family relationships where possible
**Action:** Reported suspected elder abuse to authorities, supported client through legal processes, liaised with Public Guardian and banking institutions to secure accounts, and facilitated family mediation sessions
**Result:** Recovered $12,000 in stolen funds, established appropriate financial safeguards, and maintained client's autonomy while ensuring ongoing family support

## Example 7: Group Work Facilitation
**Situation:** High dropout rates in domestic violence support groups with women citing lack of childcare and transport as barriers
**Task:** Redesign group program to improve accessibility and retention rates
**Action:** Established on-site childcare with qualified educators, negotiated transport vouchers with local council, moved sessions to community centre with better public transport access, and introduced flexible attendance options
**Result:** Increased group retention rate from 35% to 80%, expanded program from 1 to 3 groups per week, and received positive feedback from 95% of participants on program accessibility

## Example 8: Team Leadership and Quality Improvement
**Situation:** High staff turnover (45% annually) in residential disability service impacting client care quality and team morale
**Task:** Implement strategies to improve staff retention and service quality while maintaining compliance standards
**Action:** Conducted staff exit interviews to identify issues, introduced peer mentoring program, negotiated additional training budget, implemented flexible rostering system, and established regular clinical supervision framework
**Result:** Reduced staff turnover to 18% within 12 months, improved client satisfaction scores by 30%, achieved 100% compliance in quality audit, and program became model for organisation's other services
