## STAR Key Selection Criteria Responses

### KSC: Experience in case management and providing direct support to vulnerable individuals experiencing homelessness or housing instability.
*(Persona: Housing Support Worker)*

#### Situation
During my role as Housing Support Worker at Inner City Housing Service, I was assigned a complex case involving a 34-year-old woman experiencing chronic homelessness with co-occurring mental health challenges and a history of domestic violence. She had been sleeping rough for 8 months after fleeing an abusive relationship, with previous unsuccessful housing attempts due to trauma responses and lack of coordinated support.

#### Task
My responsibility was to provide comprehensive case management that addressed her immediate safety needs while building her capacity for sustainable housing. This required coordinating multiple service providers, developing trust through trauma-informed practice, and creating a realistic pathway to permanent accommodation that acknowledged her complex needs and personal agency.

#### Action
I began with gentle, client-led engagement, meeting the client at locations where she felt safe rather than requiring office visits. Over 3 months, I conducted thorough psychosocial assessments using strengths-based approaches. I coordinated with mental health clinicians, domestic violence specialists, and private rental advocates to create an integrated support plan. Recognizing her love for animals as a stabilizing factor, I researched pet-friendly accommodation options and liaised with housing providers to secure temporary accommodation that could accommodate her companion dog. I provided practical support including assistance with Centrelink applications and court accompaniment. Throughout this process, I maintained detailed case notes, conducted weekly risk assessments, and adjusted strategies based on her feedback.

#### Result
After 4 months of intensive case management, the client successfully transitioned to stable community housing with ongoing floating support. Her mental health stabilized with consistent engagement with psychiatric services. Six months post-housing, she remained stably housed and had begun volunteering with a local animal rescue organization. This case reinforced my understanding that effective homelessness support requires patience, creativity, and genuine respect for client expertise.

### KSC: An ability to appropriately use lived experience of recovery to build trust and support the engagement of clients in an Alcohol and Other Drugs (AOD) setting.
*(Persona: AOD Peer Support Worker)*

#### Situation
While working as a Peer Support Worker at Recovery Pathways Centre, I encountered a 28-year-old man entering residential rehabilitation for methamphetamine dependence. He expressed significant shame and was highly resistant to engaging with clinical staff, having experienced judgmental treatment in previous service interactions. He specifically requested to speak with someone who "actually understood."

#### Task
My role was to provide peer support that drew appropriately on my lived experience of addiction and recovery while maintaining professional boundaries. I needed to help him feel understood and reduce his sense of isolation, while supporting his engagement with the broader treatment team.

#### Action
I began by sharing appropriately boundaried aspects of my own recovery journey, focusing on universal themes like the shame cycle and the difficulty of asking for help. I emphasized that while our experiences shared common elements, his journey was uniquely his own. I used my lived experience to normalize his feelings of ambivalence about treatment while gently challenging hopelessness. I modeled how to engage constructively with clinical staff and facilitated connections with peer support groups. Importantly, I regularly debriefed with my supervisor to ensure my own recovery remained protected while I provided effective support.

#### Result
Over 6 weeks, the client transitioned from reluctant participant to active engagement in his treatment program. He began attending group sessions and developed a meaningful relationship with his assigned clinician. He later shared that having someone who "got it" without judgment was crucial to his willingness to be vulnerable. He successfully completed the 12-week program and remained engaged with outpatient services.

### KSC: Demonstrated organisational skills, including the ability to contribute to project planning, stakeholder communication, and report drafting.
*(Persona: Community Project Officer)*

#### Situation
As Community Project Officer at Westside Community Partnership, I was tasked with coordinating the "Bridges to Belonging" community engagement initiative. This was a 12-month project with a $65,000 budget, 6 partner organizations, and deliverables including 8 community events and quarterly reports to three different funding bodies.

#### Task
I needed to develop comprehensive project management systems that ensured timely delivery of all commitments while maintaining quality engagement with diverse stakeholders. This required creating clear communication protocols, robust budget tracking, and reporting frameworks.

#### Action
I began by developing a detailed project charter using collaborative planning sessions. I created a comprehensive stakeholder communication matrix, scheduling bi-weekly partner meetings and monthly volunteer check-ins. Using project management software, I tracked all deliverables, budget expenditure, and risk factors with weekly reviews. For reporting, I developed templates that streamlined data collection, creating one master dataset that could be adapted for various funder needs. I maintained detailed financial records with monthly reconciliation.

#### Result
The project exceeded all key performance indicators, delivering 10 community events (25% above target) and engaging 340 participants (70% above projected numbers). All quarterly reports were submitted on time with positive feedback from funders. Budget management resulted in a 3% underspend, which was reallocated to extend volunteer training programs. The project's success led to additional funding for Phase 2.
