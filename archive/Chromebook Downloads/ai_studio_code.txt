## STAR KSC Responses

### KSC: Experience in providing direct support to vulnerable individuals
*(Persona: Housing Support Worker)*

**Situation:** In my role at a homelessness outreach service, I was assigned a client who had been rough sleeping for over two years and had a deep distrust of support services due to past negative experiences.
**Task:** My primary task was to build rapport and trust with the client, with the ultimate goal of connecting them to stable housing and mental health support.
**Action:** I employed a trauma-informed approach, meeting the client on their terms in familiar public spaces rather than an office. I used active listening and consistently followed through on small promises, like bringing a coffee or a warm blanket. Over three weeks, I mapped local services and, with the client's gradual consent, facilitated a warm handover to a community mental health practitioner.
**Result:** This patient, client-centered approach successfully broke the cycle of disengagement. The client is now housed in supportive accommodation and actively participates in their mental health recovery plan.

### KSC: Ability to use lived experience of recovery
*(Persona: AOD Peer Support Worker)*

**Situation:** A new client in our AOD recovery program was expressing significant hopelessness, stating that they didn't believe long-term recovery was possible for them.
**Task:** My task was to leverage my own lived experience to instill a sense of hope and model the possibility of a positive future, without oversharing or making the conversation about myself.
**Action:** I used intentional self-disclosure, briefly sharing that I had faced similar feelings of hopelessness early in my own journey. I then quickly refocused the conversation on them, using motivational interviewing techniques to explore their own values and what a life in recovery could look like for them. I shared practical, non-clinical strategies that had helped me navigate early cravings.
**Result:** The client reported a significant shift in perspective, stating that speaking with someone "who gets it" was a turning point. They remained engaged in the program and later became a mentor for newer members.

### KSC: Demonstrated organisational skills
*(Persona: Community Project Officer)*

**Situation:** Our annual community wellness event, which serves over 300 residents, was at risk due to a last-minute withdrawal of a key service provider.
**Task:** I was tasked with assisting the Program Coordinator to find a replacement provider, update all event materials, and communicate the change to all stakeholders within a 48-hour timeframe.
**Action:** I immediately created a shared spreadsheet to track potential replacement providers, their contact details, and our communication status. I drafted a clear and concise email template to send to stakeholders. While the Coordinator made the calls, I updated the event run sheet and promotional materials in real-time.
**Result:** Due to our systematic and parallel approach, we secured a new provider within 24 hours and successfully updated and distributed all communications with a day to spare. The event proceeded smoothly with no negative impact on attendees.
