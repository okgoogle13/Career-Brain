ATS Resume Formatting Checklist

File Format & Structure:

[ ] Save as .docx or PDF (text-based): 

Avoid image-based PDFs.

If possible, use .docx.

[ ] Simple, Single-Column Layout: 

Avoid tables, text boxes, and multiple columns.

[ ] Standard Section Headings: 

Use clear headings like "Summary," "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills."

Avoid creative or unusual headings.

[ ] Consistent Date Format: 

Use MM/YYYY or Month YYYY.

[ ] 1-inch Margins: 

Ensure adequate white space.

[ ] Avoid Headers and Footers: 

ATS systems often have issues reading these.

Text & Content:

[ ] Simple, Standard Fonts: 

Use Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, or similar.

Font size: 11-12pt for body text, 14-16pt for headings.

[ ] Use Bullet Points: 

To break up text and make it easy to scan.

[ ] Relevant Keywords: 

Incorporate keywords from the job description naturally.

Use the same terminology as the job posting.

[ ] Plain Text: 

Avoid special characters, symbols, or graphics.

Spell out acronyms, at least the first time that they are used.

[ ] Clear and Concise Language: 

Avoid jargon, and use simple, direct language.

General Best Practices:

[ ] Proofread Carefully: 

Check for spelling and grammar errors.

[ ] Tailor Your Resume: 

Customize your resume for each job application.

[ ] Chronological Order: 

Use reverse chronological order in the work experience section.

[ ] Contact Information: 

Ensure that your contact information is correct.

Key Considerations:

ATS systems are designed to parse text. Therefore, prioritizing clear, simple text-based formatting is crucial.

Keywords are essential for getting your resume noticed by ATS. Carefully analyze job descriptions to identify relevant keywords.

By following this checklist, you'll significantly increase your chances of your resume passing through ATS and reaching human eyes.
