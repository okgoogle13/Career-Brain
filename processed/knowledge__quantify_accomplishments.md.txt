Use Numbers and Statistics:
Instead of saying "assisted clients," state "provided direct support to 50 clients per month" or "increased client access to resources by 15%". 

Focus on Outcomes:
Instead of saying "developed a program," state "developed and implemented a program that resulted in a 20% reduction in recidivism rates". 

Highlight Specific Achievements:
Instead of saying "managed a team," state "managed a team of 10, increasing productivity by 20% within six months". 

Quantify Your Impact on Clients:
Mention the number of clients helped, the types of services provided, and the positive outcomes achieved. 

Showcase Your Skills:
Quantify your skills by mentioning the number of cases you handled, the complexity of the cases, and the types of interventions you used. 

Tailor Your Resume:
Customize your resume to match the specific requirements and keywords of each job description. 

Use Action Verbs:
Start your accomplishment statements with strong action verbs that demonstrate your accomplishments, such as "implemented," "developed," "managed," "increased," or "improved". 

Be Specific and Measurable:
Provide concrete examples of your achievements and the impact you made. 

Showcase your specialization areas:
Many social workers specialize in certain areas, such as child welfare, mental health, or substance abuse. Make sure to prominently feature your specialization(s) on your resume, as it shows your expertise and passion.
