## STAR Key Selection Criteria Responses

### Housing Support Case Management Response

#### Situation Context

During my role as Housing Support Worker at Inner City Housing Service, I was assigned a complex case involving Sarah, a 34-year-old woman experiencing chronic homelessness with co-occurring mental health challenges and a history of domestic violence. She had been sleeping rough for 8 months after fleeing an abusive relationship, with previous unsuccessful housing attempts due to trauma responses and lack of coordinated support.

#### Task Requirements

My responsibility was to provide comprehensive case management that addressed Sarah's immediate safety needs while building her capacity for sustainable housing. This required coordinating multiple service providers, developing trust through trauma-informed practice, and creating a realistic pathway to permanent accommodation that acknowledged her complex needs and personal agency.

#### Action Implementation

I began with gentle, client-led engagement, meeting Sarah at locations where she felt safe rather than requiring office visits. Over 3 months, I conducted thorough psychosocial assessments using strengths-based approaches, identifying her resilience factors and support preferences. I coordinated with mental health clinicians, domestic violence specialists, and private rental advocates to create an integrated support plan. Recognizing Sarah's love for animals as a stabilizing factor, I researched pet-friendly accommodation options and liaised with housing providers to secure temporary accommodation that could accommodate her companion dog. I provided practical support including assistance with Centrelink applications, court accompaniment for intervention orders, and gentle advocacy with potential landlords. Throughout this process, I maintained detailed case notes, conducted weekly risk assessments, and adjusted strategies based on Sarah's feedback and changing circumstances.

#### Result Achievement

After 4 months of intensive case management, Sarah successfully transitioned to stable community housing with ongoing floating support. Her mental health stabilized with consistent engagement with psychiatric services, and she developed confidence in maintaining tenancy through our ongoing support relationship. Six months post-housing, Sarah remained stably housed and had begun volunteering with a local animal rescue organization. This case reinforced my understanding that effective homelessness support requires patience, creativity, and genuine respect for client expertise about their own lives and needs.

### AOD Peer Support Lived Experience Response

#### Situation Context

While working as a Peer Support Worker at Recovery Pathways Centre, I encountered James, a 28-year-old man entering residential rehabilitation for methamphetamine dependence. James expressed significant shame about his substance use and was highly resistant to engaging with clinical staff, having experienced judgmental treatment in previous service interactions. He specifically requested to speak with someone who "actually understood" rather than "just read about it in textbooks."

#### Task Requirements

My role was to provide peer support that drew appropriately on my lived experience of addiction and recovery while maintaining professional boundaries. I needed to help James feel understood and reduce his sense of isolation, while supporting his engagement with the broader treatment team and avoiding the pitfall of assuming our experiences were identical.

#### Action Implementation

I began by sharing appropriately boundaried aspects of my own recovery journey, focusing on universal themes like the shame cycle and the difficulty of asking for help, rather than specific drug-use details. I emphasized that while our experiences shared common elements, his journey was uniquely his own. I used my lived experience to normalize his feelings of ambivalence about treatment while gently challenging hopelessness. During our sessions, I modeled how to engage constructively with clinical staff, sharing how I had learned to advocate for my needs within treatment settings. I facilitated connections with peer support groups and helped James identify personal recovery values that went beyond simply stopping drug use. Importantly, I regularly debriefed with my supervisor about any emotional responses triggered by James's story, ensuring my own recovery remained protected while I provided effective support.

#### Result Achievement

Over 6 weeks, James transitioned from reluctant participant to active engagement in his treatment program. He began attending group sessions, developed a meaningful relationship with his assigned clinician, and started planning for post-residential support. James later shared that having someone who "got it" without judgment was crucial to his willingness to be vulnerable in treatment. He successfully completed the 12-week program and remained engaged with outpatient services. This experience reinforced my understanding that lived experience, when shared skillfully and appropriately, can be a powerful tool for reducing stigma and building therapeutic relationships, while always maintaining focus on the client's unique journey and needs.

### Community Project Organizational Skills Response

#### Situation Context

As Community Project Officer at Westside Community Partnership, I was tasked with coordinating the "Bridges to Belonging" community engagement initiative. This was a 12-month project aimed at improving social connection among newly arrived refugees and migrants. The project involved a $65,000 budget, 6 partner organizations, 15 community volunteers, and deliverables including 8 community events, a resource directory, and quarterly reports to three different funding bodies with varying requirements.

#### Task Requirements

I needed to develop comprehensive project management systems that ensured timely delivery of all commitments while maintaining quality engagement with diverse stakeholders. This required creating clear communication protocols, robust budget tracking, risk management processes, and reporting frameworks that satisfied different funder requirements while genuinely reflecting community impact.

#### Action Implementation

I began by developing a detailed project charter using collaborative planning sessions with partner organizations, establishing clear roles, timelines, and success metrics. I created a comprehensive stakeholder communication matrix, scheduling bi-weekly partner meetings, monthly volunteer check-ins, and quarterly steering committee updates. Using project management software, I tracked all deliverables, budget expenditure, and risk factors with weekly reviews. I established culturally responsive evaluation methods, including translated feedback forms and informal community conversations, ensuring participant voices were genuinely captured. For reporting, I developed templates that streamlined data collection across different requirements, creating one master dataset that could be adapted for various funder needs. I maintained detailed financial records with monthly reconciliation and implemented approval processes for all expenditure. When the COVID-19 pandemic required pivoting from in-person to hybrid delivery models, I facilitated rapid consultation with communities and partners, redesigning activities while maintaining project integrity and budget parameters.

#### Result Achievement

The project exceeded all key performance indicators, delivering 10 community events, which was 25% above target, engaging 340 participants, representing 70% above projected numbers, and completing the resource directory in 4 languages. All quarterly reports were submitted on time with positive feedback from funders about clarity and comprehensiveness. Budget management resulted in 3% underspend, which was reallocated to extend volunteer training programs. Partner feedback surveys averaged 4.7 out of 5 for project coordination effectiveness. The project's success led to additional funding for Phase 2, with funders specifically requesting I continue as Project Officer. This experience demonstrated my capacity to manage complex, multi-stakeholder initiatives while maintaining focus on genuine community benefit and rigorous accountability to funders.
