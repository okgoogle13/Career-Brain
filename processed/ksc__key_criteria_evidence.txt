# Key Selection Criteria Evidence Strategy

## 1. Passion for Peer-Based, Drug-Related Harm Reduction and Health Promotion

### **Career Trajectory Evidence:**
- **Values-driven career change**: Left lucrative finance sector (9+ years) specifically due to "values misalignment" - shows genuine commitment over financial incentive
- **Sustained engagement**: 4+ years continuous involvement in harm reduction work across multiple organizations
- **Progressive responsibility**: Evolved from volunteer → peer worker → community support worker → multicultural practice intern

### **Concrete Examples to Highlight:**
- "Transitioned from Senior Project Manager earning £X to volunteer peer worker - demonstrating authentic passion over profit"
- Led bi-weekly peer support groups for 13 months, consistently showing up for community
- Delivered 2,000+ care packages during COVID - going above and beyond when community needed it most
- Currently pursuing Diploma of Community Services while working - investing in professional development
- Specific interest in HRVic mentioned in resume shows targeted passion

### **Impact Metrics:**
- 400+ vulnerable clients supported with tailored harm reduction approaches
- 36 international students engaged in culturally sensitive mental health resource development
- Created resources approved for national rollout - scalable impact

## 2. Lived or Living Experience of Substance Use

### **Authentic Language to Use:**
- "Lived experience navigating the complexities of AOD issues both as someone seeking support and providing it"
- "Personal understanding of stigma, judgment, and shame faced by people who use drugs whilst navigating services"
- "Currently participating in novel psychoactive substance trials for treatment-resistant depression"

### **Professional Integration Evidence:**
- SMART Recovery facilitator training - shows you've processed your lived experience professionally
- Transitioned from "seeking peer support" to "providing peer support" - demonstrates recovery journey
- Able to leverage lived experience while maintaining professional boundaries (evidenced by successful roles)

### **Specific Examples:**
- "My personal journey with substance use informs my understanding of client barriers and motivations"
- "I understand the difference between harm reduction theory and lived reality"
- "My ongoing therapeutic relationship with novel substances provides current, authentic perspective"

## 3. Knowledge of Drug Markets, Trends and Emerging Novel Psychoactive Substances

### **Unique Positioning:**
- **Current trial participation**: Real-time, supervised experience with novel psychoactive substances
- **Professional + personal insight**: Understands both clinical/therapeutic applications AND recreational contexts
- **Staying current**: Ongoing engagement means up-to-date knowledge of emerging compounds

### **Evidence to Emphasize:**
- "Current participation in novel psychoactive substance trials provides cutting-edge understanding of emerging compounds and their effects"
- "Integrated understanding of pharmacokinetics gained through both lived experience and professional practice"
- "Sound understanding of drug effects, medication interactions, and poly-substance use patterns"
- "Awareness of trends in novel psychoactive substances gained through personal journey and community engagement"

### **Specific Knowledge Areas:**
- Therapeutic vs recreational applications of psychoactive substances
- Drug interactions and contraindications
- Emerging treatment-resistant depression protocols
- Community trends observed through peer work at venues and support groups

## **Strategic Positioning:**

### **What Makes You Unique:**
1. **Dual perspective**: Both seeking and providing support - rare authentic insight
2. **Current engagement**: Not just past experience - ongoing, evolving understanding
3. **Professional processing**: Haven't just experienced - have transformed experience into professional competency
4. **Values-driven**: Demonstrated through major career pivot and sustained commitment
5. **Scalable impact**: From individual peer support to national resource development

### **Language to Avoid:**
- Don't be overly clinical about lived experience
- Don't minimize the significance of your trial participation
- Don't undersell the rarity of your dual perspective

### **Language to Embrace:**
- "Authentic peer connection"
- "Lived reality of substance use"
- "Real-time understanding"
- "Values-driven commitment"
- "Integrated professional and personal knowledge"
