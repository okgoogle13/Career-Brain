W hat is a tailoredresume?
A tailored resume is a
customized resume that
matches the specific
requirements of your target
role and company. Unlike a

m aster resum e that stores all
your experience, a tailored
version selectively highlights
relevant qualifications that
prove you're the ideal
candidate.
This means:
Using language from the job
description
Emphasizing relevant
responsibilities
Incorporating company-
specific details from your
research
This targeted approach
increases your visibility with
hiring managers and helps
you stand out from other
candidates.
 
5 benefits oftailoring yourresume to the jobdescription
Tailoring your resume for each
job description might require
some effort, but it comes with
substantial rewards. A tailored
resume:
 
1. Increases your chances of
an interview: W hen you
customize your resume for a
specific job description, you're
speaking directly to the hiring
manager's needs. Your resume
becomes the solution to their

problem. By showcasing your
skills and experiences that
directly align with the job
requirements, you significantly
up your chances of landing an
interview invitation.
2. Optimizes for Applicant
Tracking Systems (ATS):
Behind the scenes, companies
use ATS to sort applications
through keyword searches of
past titles and specific
qualifications. Incorporating
relevant terms will make your
resume more discoverable to
hiring teams.
3. Highlights relevant
qualifications: Your resume
should reflect the top 10% of
your qualifications that are
relevant to the role you're
applying to. W hen you modify
your content for each
employer's job description,
you spotlight your most
relevant qualifications for that
job—helping get your resum e
noticed.
4. Demonstrates
understanding of the role:
Adapting your resume to fit a
specific job demonstrates
your depth of understanding
of the role. A tailored resume
is a clear sign that you've done
your homework and grasp the
intricacies of the position and
company.

5. Sets you apart from other
applicants: In a pool brimming
with talent, you want to be the
one who stands out. Tailoring
your resume helps you do
exactly that. It underscores
your genuine interest and
investment in the position,
attention to detail, and effort
to set you apart from those
candidates sending the same
resume to every company.

2. Research the
company
Looking into the company
you're applying at, what they
do, and what they stand for is
crucial. Knowing these facts
during the interview process
makes a massive difference.
As a bonus, it's not as
expected in the application
process, so understanding the
mission, values, and vision can
help you stand out.
Here's how you can do this:
Look at the company
website
Check out its social
channels
Zoom in on their core values
if they have them publicly
available
Search for a video about the
company and its product
Pro Tip:  Search for a podcast
that includes the Founder or
CEO. Hearing how they
describe the company and
what they stand for can help
you get a feel for their
messaging. 
 
3. Focus on the top
half of your resume

The top half of your resume is
the most important because
it's where recruiters and hiring
managers look first. A
customized summary with
relevant skills and targeted
resum e achievements here
proves you understand what
matters for this role and shows
your value quickly.
Keep this section concise,
well-organized, and visually
appealing - using bullet
points, headings, and clear
language to highlight key
qualifications. This first
impression demonstrates
you're a serious candidate
who's done their homework
and encourages recruiters to
keep reading.
 
4. Adjust target job
title
 W hen it comes to the target
title of your resume, you might
think this should reflect the
position you currently hold or
held in your last role, but not
necessarily.
The target title of your resume
should align as closely as
possible with the specific job
description of the role you're
applying for. So that means
each time you fill out an
application, you may need to
tweak your target to fit the

specific role the hiring
manager is looking for. (For
example, titles like Copywriter,
Content W riter, and Content
Marketer can have many of
the same requirements, but
the job titles slightly differ.) 
 
5. Tailor your summ ary
Your professional summary
gives readers a quick overview
of your experience and top
skills. Take the key
requirements you've identified
and:
Lead with skills and
achievements that directly
match their needs
Mirror the job posting's
language and priorities
Emphasize experiences
most relevant to this role
 
6. Customize your
achievements
Your work experience section
should highlight
accomplishments that directly
relate to your target role.
For each role you're applying
to:
Select achievements that
demonstrate relevant skills
Quantify results where
possible
Focus on impact that
matters for the new position

 
7. Match your resume
to job description
keywords
W hen recruiters search their
ATS for candidates, they look
for specific resum e keywords
from the job description. Make
your resume discoverable by
weaving these terms naturally
throughout - especially in your
summary, achievements, and
skills sections. Don't stuff
keywords; instead, use them
thoughtfully to describe your
actual experience and
capabilities.
This approach not only helps
you get found but also shows
hiring managers you
understand their needs and
have relevant experience.
