## PART 1: Victorian Government Sector Glossary & Inclusive Language Guide
*(Harvested from Victorian Department of Families, Fairness and Housing (DFFH) Jan 2025 Strategy & Transgender Victoria (TGV) 2024)*

### A. Core Acronyms & Categories
*   **LGBTIQA+**: Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Trans and gender diverse, Intersex, Queer, Questioning, Asexual (with the "+" representing other fluid sexualities and gender identities).
    *   *The "A" Addition*: Explicitly represents **Asexual** (experiencing partial or no sexual attraction, regardless of gender or romantic orientation).
*   **TGD**: Transgender and Gender Diverse.
*   **TGDNB**: Trans, Gender Diverse, and Non-Binary (the standard acronym for VPS workforce reporting).
*   **QTIBIPOC**: Queer, Trans, Intersex, Black, and Indigenous People of Colour (focuses on compound, intersecting marginalization).
*   **Endosex**: People whose physical or biological sex characteristics meet typical medical/social norms for male or female bodies (opposite of Intersex variations).

### B. First Nations Cultural Gender Identities
*   **Sistergirl**: A term used by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities to describe transgender or gender-diverse people who embody a female spirit and take on female roles within their mob (such as caring for children, families, and community).
*   **Brotherboy**: A term used to describe gender-diverse people who embody a male spirit and take on male roles in the community.
*   **Transmob**: An Indigenous-led term of endearment and identification for trans, gender-diverse, and non-binary First Nations people.
*   > [!IMPORTANT]
    > **Contextual Dual-Use**: In broader Aboriginal communities, the terms "sistagirl" and "brothaboy" are also used as standard terms of endearment for biological sisters/brothers or close friends without any reference to gender diversity. Interpret these terms carefully within context.

### C. Psychological & Affirmation Concepts
*   **Affirmation**: The act of validating, supporting, and respecting someone's gender identity or expression (legal, social, or medical).
*   **Gender Dysphoria**: The distress arising from incongruence between experienced gender and sex assigned at birth.
*   **Gender Euphoria**: The deep joy, relief, and contentment arising from gender-affirming experiences and self-actualization.
*   **Misgendering**: Addressing or referring to someone using language, names, or pronouns that do not align with their gender identity.
*   **Deadnaming**: Referencing or using a trans person's birth name without their explicit consent.

### D. Systemic & Values Frameworks
*   **The Intersectional Approach**: An analytical model that rejects monolithic assessments. It evaluates how multiple overlapping factors (such as gender, sexual orientation, disability, race, age, and class) combine to shape unique experiences of privilege and discrimination. (e.g., policy analysis must address differences in barriers faced by trans women of color compared to white trans people with disabilities).
*   **Victorian Public Sector (VPS) Core Values**:
    1.  **Responsiveness**: Delivering highly capability-driven, timely, and supportive service.
    2.  **Integrity**: Maintaining honest, transparent, ethical, and anti-oppressive practice.
    3.  **Impartiality**: Providing objective, equitable, and evidence-based services.
    4.  **Accountability**: Accepting responsibility for decisions and outcomes.
    5.  **Respect**: Treating all colleagues, clients, and community members with fairness and dignity.
    6.  **Leadership**: Modeling proactive behaviors and inspiring collaborative, multi-disciplinary excellence.

## PART 3: The Few-Shot STAR / SAO / CAR Library

Use the following real-world example snippets to calibrate the LLM's tone, pacing, structural flow, and word counts.

### A. Word Count & Structural Guidelines
*   **SAO Approach (Situation, Action, Outcome)**: Endorsed by the Victorian Government as a high-density Claim statement.
*   **The 60–120 Word Rule**: Snippets intended for resume experience descriptions or quick cover letter callouts must stay strictly within this limit to prevent recruiter panel fatigue.
*   **The 150–250 Word Rule**: In-depth claims for selection criteria must structure each STAR/CAR segment to fit this range, keeping sentences dense with metrics and action verbs.

### B. Gold Standard Snippets

#### 1. Youth Case Work & Challenging Behaviours (STAR Framework)
*   **Situation**: I managed the case of a 12-year-old youth diagnosed with ADHD experiencing severe behavioral challenges (truancy, public tantrums, medication non-compliance) disrupting home and school life.
*   **Task**: To secure his trust, establish a daily medication compliance routine, and support behavior modification.
*   **Action**: I established a collaborative, gamified reward system points model to motivate daily compliance. I co-designed a unique multi-modal communication framework (incorporating art, puzzles, and a personalized sign language we co-created) to facilitate active engagement and emotional regulation.
*   **Result**: Secured 100% daily medication compliance, stabilized school attendance, and reduced domestic behavioral outbursts by 80% within 3 months.
*   *Competency*: Relationship Management / Working with Vulnerable Populations.

#### 2. Technical, Financial & Operational Problem-Solving (SAO Framework)
*   **Situation**: While working as a Business Analyst at Sydney Water, lease audits revealed government rooms hired out to private sectors had rental structures that failed to reflect market capacity, size, or facilities.
*   **Action**: I conducted a rigorous multi-variant database analysis on all facility spreadsheets and recommended targeted rent adjustments of 20% to 75%. During this audit, I discovered a long-term recurring room booking that had never been entered into the billing database. I immediately initiated a system-wide reconciliation, set up the user in the billing engine, and negotiated terms for back-payment.
*   **Outcome**: Successfully recovered two years of back-payments without client attrition, resulting in a permanent recurring revenue boost for the department.
*   *Competency*: Technical Literacy & Strategic Problem Solving.

#### 3. Equity, Diversity & Complex Needs (CAR Framework)
*   **Context (Situation/Task)**: At Royal North Shore Hospital, I was tasked with addressing employment barriers for underrepresented communities and facilitating inclusive workforce pathways.
*   **Action**: I directed the hospital's Equity and Diversity program, building strong networks across training providers and community employment agencies. I designed and rolled out customized work placement and traineeship programs tailored for mature-age women returning to the workforce, disability support seekers, and Indigenous youth. I personally collaborated with departmental managers to implement reasonable adjustments in the workspace.
*   **Result**: Created 45+ inclusive traineeship placements in 12 months, achieving an 85% long-term workforce retention rate and successfully fostering a culturally safe workplace.
*   *Competency*: Diversity & Stakeholder Engagement.

#### 4. Customer Service De-escalation (SAO Snippet - 90 words)
*   **Situation**: As a Customer Complaints Officer at Acme Department Stores, I managed highly complex escalations (average 3 per week) where customers were highly agitated.
*   **Action**: I actively listened, de-escalated emotions using trauma-informed communication, investigated the breakdown from both staff and customer perspectives, and negotiated mutually agreeable resolutions.
*   **Outcome**: Resolved 99% of complaints directly without managerial escalation, earning consistent commendations from executive leadership for speed and sensitivity.

## PART 4: Cover Letter (CAO) & Resume Opening Templates

To ensure a hook that creates immediate "emotional resonance," the LLM must adopt these opening and structural formulas.

### A. The CAO (Context-Action-Outcome) Cover Letter Structure
*   **Opening Hook (3-5 sentences)**: Connects the candidate's career pivot/lived experience directly to the organization's mission, using the Rosetta Stone translation.
*   **Sentence Starter Templates**:
    *   *Introduction (Passion)*: "It was my first-hand experience with [Harm Reduction / System Advocacy] that inspired me to apply for the [Role Title] position at [Organisation]. Having witnessed the profound impact of [Values/Practice], I am highly motivated to bring my specialized capability to your team."
    *   *Introduction (Accomplishment)*: "Throughout my [X] years of experience in [Service Coordination / Quality Assurance], I have prided myself on translating complex regulatory constraints into client-centered support structures. I am eager to apply this hybrid expertise to [Organisation's] upcoming goals."
    *   *Body (The Bridge)*: "My previous success in [Rosetta Stone Corporate Area, e.g., Financial Risk Modelling] has proven my capability in [Rosetta Stone Community Equivalent, e.g., MARAM Risk Assessment & Safety Planning]. I can contribute directly to your team's effectiveness by..."
    *   *Closing (Call to Action)*: "I welcome the opportunity to discuss my qualifications and my deep alignment with [Organisation's] commitment to [Trauma-Informed Care / Social Justice] in a personal interview. Thank you for your time and consideration."
